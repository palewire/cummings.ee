e.e. cummings free poetry archive

the moon is hiding in
her hair,
The
lily
of heaven
full of all dreams,
drawns down.

cover her briefness in singing
close her with intricate faint birds
by daisies and twilights
Deepen her,

Recite
upon her
flesh
the rain’s

pearls singly-whispering

This poem was first published as “Post Impressions II” in “Tulips and Chimneys.” The previous poem is “Post Impressions I.” The next poem is “Post Impressions III.”
